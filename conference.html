Conference Talks

I like to present my on going research work in conferences. For the past three years my work was selected for presentation at the conferences. In particular, I have been actively presenting my work at Agricultural & Applied Economics Association (AAEA) and Western Agricultural Economics Association (WAEA) annual meetings.

A Dynamic model of U.S. beef Cattle

I presented my initial work of the dynamic model at the 2020 AAEA annual meeting. This was a virtual presentation. My presentation included the development of the dynamic model and initial calibration of the model under Naïve price expectations. The comments and feedback I got from the conference are used to improve the model. Currently the finished model is calibrated under both Naïve price expectations and Rational price expectations.

Implementing a National Animal Identification and Traceability Program: Economic Assessment using a Dynamic Model of U.S. Beef Cattle

This work was presented as a poster at 2021 AAEA annual meeting in Austin, Texas. The work was received well by many graduate students and researchers. I had a good discussion with multiple researchers from different universities in the United States.

A Dynamic Assessment of The Economic Impacts of A Foot-and-Mouth Disease Outbreak on the U.S. Beef Cattle Industry

This paper is selected as a paper presentation at 2022 WAEA annual meeting in Santa Fe, New Mexico.
